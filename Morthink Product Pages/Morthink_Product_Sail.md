### Section 2: What Sail Protects Against

**Header:** What Sail Blocks

*[Image: Heat entering glass vs. Sail film blocking heat]*

Sail keeps heat out without blocking the driver's view. Three VLT options let the buyer choose how much light to let in. The heat rejection benefit increases as the VLT decreases.

**Heat Rejection**

*[Image: Interior temperature comparison with and without film]*

Sail rejects solar energy before it passes through the glass. Less heat enters the cabin. The air conditioning system works less hard. The interior environment is more comfortable on hot days.

**UV Rejection**

*[Image: UV rejection diagram or UV-faded interior vs. protected interior]*

Sail rejects 99 percent or more of UV light across all three models. This protects the vehicle interior from fading and reduces UV exposure to the driver and passengers.

**Glare Reduction**

*[Image: Driver's view with and without glare]*

Lower VLT models reduce glare from direct sunlight and oncoming headlights. The film does not create visible ghosting or double-image on the windshield.

### Section 3: How Sail Performs

**Header:** How Sail Performs After Install

**Factory Matching Reflectivity**

*[Image: Vehicle with Sail installed showing exterior reflectivity matching factory glass]*

Sail is built to match factory glass reflectivity from outside the vehicle. The installed result does not make the car look heavily tinted or add visible hotspots. The exterior appearance stays close to stock.

**Clear View from Inside**

*[Image: Interior view through Sail-installed glass showing clarity]*

Haze across all three Sail models stays low. The driver's view through the glass remains clear. The film does not scatter light or create a hazy interior experience.

**Consistent Performance Across VLT Options**

*[Image: Three VLT variants side by side on glass panels]*

The DS75, DS35, and DS15 models use the same nano-ceramic platform. The construction is consistent. Only the light transmission level changes between models, not the underlying quality of the film.
