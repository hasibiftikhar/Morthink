### Section 2: What Choyu Protects Against

**Header:** What Choyu Protects Against

*[Image: Stone chip or scratch impact on unprotected paint vs. Choyu-protected surface]*

Choyu is built to do the job paint protection film is supposed to do. It takes scratches, stone chips, and surface contamination so the paint underneath does not have to.

**Scratches and Stone Chips**

*[Image: Close-up of chip or scratch absorbed by film surface]*

The film surface handles impact from road debris. Minor scratches and stone chip strikes stay on the film. The paint underneath stays untouched.

**Self Healing**

*[Image: Scratch disappearing from film surface under heat or sunlight]*

Light scratches on the film surface heal on their own. Sun exposure or a warm water rinse activates the coating. Swirl marks and minor surface marks disappear without polishing or additional labor.

**Yellowing and Hydrolysis Resistance**

*[Image: White vehicle with Choyu showing no yellowing over time]*

Choyu resists yellowing and hydrolysis. White cars stay white. The film holds its clarity from the day it is installed through years of daily use.

**Surface Contamination**

*[Image: Water beading or contaminants rolling off hydrophobic surface]*

The surface coating resists water spots and environmental fouling. Contaminants have a harder time bonding to the surface, so the film stays cleaner between washes.

### Section 3: How Choyu Performs

**Header:** How Choyu Performs During and After Install

**Easy to Apply**

*[Image: Installer applying Choyu film to a panel]*

Choyu is engineered for clean, straightforward installation. The coating elongation at break on the SW75 model is 150 percent, giving installers enough working room to handle real vehicle panels without over stretching the film. Initial tack of 20 N per 25mm holds the film in place during positioning.

**Paint Safe Adhesive**

*[Image: Clean film removal showing no residue on paint]*

The adhesive holds firmly during installation and in daily use. When the film needs to come off, it releases cleanly. No marks, no residue on the original paint.

**UV Protection**

*[Image: UV rejection diagram or side-by-side comparison]*

Choyu rejects 95 percent of UV light across both models. This protects the paint underneath from fading and keeps the film itself from degrading under prolonged sun exposure.

**Optical Clarity (SW75)**

*[Image: Clear vehicle with Choyu showing paint color true to original]*

The SW75 gloss model delivers 95 GU gloss and 90.1 percent visible light transmission. The paint color you see on install day is the paint color your customer sees every day.

**Matte Finish (LW75M14)**

*[Image: Vehicle with matte finish Choyu showing even, consistent matte surface]*

The LW75M14 model delivers a 14 GU deep matte finish. It gives customers who want a factory matte look, or a matte wrap appearance, a cost-effective route to get it with protection built in.
