### Section 2: Paint Protection Film

**Header:** Paint Protection Film

**What is paint protection film?**

Paint protection film, also called PPF, is a clear or colored film applied directly to a vehicle's painted surfaces. It sits between the paint and the outside world. Stone chips, minor scratches, bird droppings, and road debris hit the film instead of the paint. When the film is eventually removed, the original paint underneath is untouched.

**What makes MoShiEx PPF different from other brands?**

MoShiEx PPF is built on the AEGOR platform, which brings together expertise from three technical backgrounds: TPU casting, adhesive science, and coating development. The result is a film where every layer is engineered specifically for the others, not sourced separately and assembled. Every batch is tested in-house and by three international collaborative laboratories before it ships. Every batch ships with a Certificate of Analysis.

**What does 280% elongation mean?**

Elongation at break is how far the film can stretch before it tears. A film with 280% elongation can be stretched to nearly four times its original length without failing. In practical terms, this means the installer can pull the film around curved panels, over bumpers, and into tight edges without the film splitting during application. The higher the elongation, the more coverage a skilled installer can achieve with a single piece of film.

**How does self healing work?**

The self healing coating on the film surface responds to heat. When a light scratch or swirl mark happens, the coating material flows back into place as it warms up. Sun exposure on a warm day is usually enough. A warm water rinse also works. Deep cuts that go through the coating layer do not heal. But minor surface marks from washing, light contact, or road debris disappear without polishing.

**How fast does MoShiEx PPF self heal?**

Healing speed varies by model. The YL75 (Lhote) and most GODEN and CHOMO models heal in 15 seconds under proper heat. Malu and Kanga models heal in 12 to 15 seconds depending on the variant. Choyu models heal in 21 to 22 seconds. These figures are measured at the manufacturer's standard test conditions.

**Will MoShiEx PPF yellow over time?**

No. MoShiEx PPF is formulated to resist yellowing. White cars stay white. The film holds its optical clarity under prolonged UV exposure and in humid or tropical climates. Yellowing in PPF is typically caused by poor UV resistance or hydrolysis in the film formulation. Both are engineered out of MoShiEx film.

**What is hydrolysis resistance?**

Hydrolysis is the chemical breakdown that happens when water and heat work on unprotected film over time. It causes film to cloud, bubble, or fail at the adhesive layer. MoShiEx PPF is engineered to resist hydrolysis. The resistance is built into the formulation, not added as a surface treatment that eventually wears off.

**Will the film damage my paint when removed?**

No, if the film is removed correctly. MoShiEx PPF uses a paint safe adhesive that grips the surface firmly during use and releases cleanly when the film is taken off. No residue is left on the paint. If a film has been installed incorrectly, has been damaged, or has been left on well past its service life without maintenance, removal can be more difficult. A trained installer should handle removal.

**Which PPF series is right for my vehicle?**

That depends on the vehicle and the installation. Lhote is for large hoods and long panels where you need 1.83m width to avoid splicing. Chomo and Goden are for premium and performance vehicles where you want the highest level of protection. Malu is the broadest line, with gloss, matte, deep black, and climate-specific variants. Kanga is for vehicles in demanding climates, including tropical heat and humidity. Choyu is the cost-effective option for buyers who want core protection without paying for premium-tier specs.

**What is the difference between a gloss and a matte PPF?**

A gloss PPF enhances the shine of the vehicle's paint underneath it. A matte PPF reduces reflectivity and gives the surface a flat, non-shiny appearance. Matte PPF can be applied over a gloss-painted vehicle to give it a factory matte look without respraying. Both types protect the paint the same way. Gloss values above 90 GU indicate a high-shine result. Gloss values below 25 GU indicate a deep matte result.

### Section 3: Window Film

**Header:** Window Film

**What is the difference between Wolfra and Magal?**

Wolfra uses magnetron sputtered metal layers. This technology achieves the highest heat rejection and the lowest reflectivity in MoShiEx's window film range. It is the premium tier. Magal uses nano-ceramic layers. Nano-ceramic film avoids the metallic appearance that some sputtered films carry and performs consistently in warm climates. Both are built to match factory glass reflectivity. Neither creates glare or ghosting on the windshield.

**What does VLT mean?**

VLT stands for visible light transmission. It is the percentage of visible light that passes through the film. A VLT of 70% means 70% of visible light gets through, leaving a lightly tinted appearance. A VLT of 15% means 15% gets through, which is a very dark tint. Legal limits for VLT vary by country and by window position on the vehicle. Check local regulations before selecting a VLT.

**What does TSER mean?**

TSER stands for total solar energy rejection. It measures how much of the sun's total energy, including visible light, infrared, and UV, the film blocks. A TSER of 63% means the film blocks 63% of incoming solar energy. Higher TSER means a cooler interior, less work for the air conditioning system, and more comfort for the driver and passengers.

**Does window film affect driving visibility?**

Applied correctly, window film should not reduce driving visibility in normal daylight conditions. MoShiEx window film maintains low haze across all models. Wolfra achieves haze of 0.6% or below. Magal achieves haze of 0.8 to 1.2% depending on the model. These are well within the range where visibility is unaffected. Night driving visibility depends on the VLT chosen. Consult a local installer about the right VLT for your conditions.

### Section 4: CHARMIST Color Film

**Header:** CHARMIST Color Film

**What is CHARMIST?**

CHARMIST is MoShiEx's colored paint protection film. It gives vehicle owners a way to change their vehicle's color and protect the paint at the same time. The base is a PPF-grade TPU substrate, the same type of material used in clear PPF. The color is a cast-grade layer bonded to the base film. It protects the original paint, can be removed without damaging it, and gives a quality color result that holds through stretching and daily use.

**How is CHARMIST different from a vinyl wrap?**

A standard vinyl wrap changes color but does not protect the paint the way PPF does. CHARMIST is built on a PPF-grade base, so it provides genuine paint protection, self healing capability, and adhesive-free removal alongside the color change. CHARMIST also uses a cast-grade color layer, meaning the color is integrated into the film and will not fade, peel away from the base, or change appearance under stretching the way a standard vinyl might.

**How many colors are available in CHARMIST?**

Over 400 colors are available. Custom color development is available on request. Color consistency across batches is managed through the DEPLOTEC proprietary color platform, so a roll ordered today matches a roll ordered six months from now.

### Section 5: Certification and Quality

**Header:** Certification and Quality

**What certifications does Morthink hold?**

Morthink holds IATF 16949 certification, the quality management standard used by the global automotive industry. We also hold ISO 9001 (quality management), ISO 14001 (environmental responsibility), and ISO 45001 (occupational health and safety). All four certifications are maintained through active surveillance audits, not one-time assessments.

**What is the AEGOR platform?**

AEGOR is the name of Morthink's internal technology and manufacturing platform. It combines 70+ years of coating technology heritage with expertise across TPU casting, adhesive science, and coating development. All MoShiEx products are designed and manufactured on this platform. It is what ensures the spec sheet values match the real-world performance of the film.

**How does Morthink test its products?**

Every product goes through three quality checks: incoming raw material testing when materials arrive at the factory, in-process inspection during manufacturing, and final quality testing before the batch ships. We also work with three international collaborative laboratories outside our own facility. These external labs provide an independent check on our processes and keep our material science current.

**What comes with every order?**

Every batch ships with a Certificate of Analysis and a full inspection report. These documents confirm that the batch has passed all three quality checks and meets the spec values on the product data sheet.
