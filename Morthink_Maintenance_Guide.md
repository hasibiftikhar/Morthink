### Section 2: Paint Protection Film Care

**Header:** Caring for Your MoShiEx PPF

**Initial Cure Period**

*[Image: Vehicle with fresh PPF install, indoors or covered]*

After installation, allow the film to cure before washing. Wait at least 48 to 72 hours before the first wash. During this period, avoid pressure washing, getting the film wet, or exposing it to heavy rain. The adhesive is still setting. Disturbing it during this window can cause edge lifting or bubbling that would not have happened otherwise.

**Regular Washing**

*[Image: Hand washing a PPF-protected vehicle with clean sponge and bucket]*

Wash the vehicle by hand using clean, pH-neutral car wash soap and a clean microfiber wash mitt. Rinse the vehicle thoroughly with water before washing to remove loose debris that could scratch the surface during the wash. Work from top to bottom. Rinse completely after washing and dry with a clean microfiber towel.

Avoid car washes with rotating brushes. Brushes trap dirt from other vehicles and drag it across the film surface. This causes light scratching over time that would not happen with a proper hand wash.

**Pressure Washing**

*[Image: Pressure washer being used at safe angle and distance on a vehicle]*

Pressure washing is acceptable with the right technique. Keep the nozzle at least 30 centimeters from the film surface. Do not direct the spray directly at film edges at close range. High-pressure spray aimed at an edge can lift the film over time. A wide-angle or fan spray nozzle is safer than a narrow jet for areas near edges.

**Self Healing Activation**

*[Image: Warm water rinse or sunlight on PPF surface showing scratch disappearing]*

Light scratches and swirl marks on the film surface heal on their own when heat is applied. Parking in direct sunlight on a warm day is usually enough to activate healing. A warm water rinse (not boiling, not freezing) also works. Run warm water over the scratched area and wait a few minutes. The mark should disappear.

Not all marks are surface scratches. A mark that does not heal after heat exposure may have gone deeper than the coating layer. Contact your installer for an assessment.

**Contamination**

*[Image: Bird droppings or tree sap on PPF surface with clean removal]*

Remove bird droppings, tree sap, and insect residue as soon as possible. These substances are acidic. Over time, they can stain or etch the coating layer if left in place. Do not scrape dried material off with a dry cloth. Soak with clean water first, then gently lift the softened residue with a damp microfiber cloth.

**What to Avoid**

*[Image: Warning visual — petroleum products, harsh solvents, abrasive cleaners]*

Do not use:
- Petroleum-based products or solvents on the film surface
- Abrasive polishes or cutting compounds
- Wax or sealant products with abrasive content
- Ammonia-based cleaners
- Rough cloths, paper towels, or sponges with scrubbing surfaces

Silicone-free wax or paint sealant products are compatible with most MoShiEx PPF. Check with your installer before applying any sealant to confirm it is safe for the specific film series installed on your vehicle.

**Edge and Seam Care**

*[Image: Film edge on panel — properly seated vs. lifted edge]*

Inspect film edges periodically. Edge lifting can happen over time, especially in areas exposed to high-speed airflow, temperature extremes, or regular water contact. An edge that has started to lift should be addressed by a trained installer before moisture gets underneath. Do not attempt to re-press lifted edges with heat guns without knowing what you are doing. Incorrect technique can damage the adhesive or the paint underneath.

### Section 3: Window Film Care

**Header:** Caring for Your MoShiEx Window Film

**Initial Cure Period**

*[Image: Interior of a vehicle with freshly installed window film]*

After installation, window film takes time to fully adhere to the glass. Do not roll down the windows for at least 48 hours after installation. Small water bubbles visible immediately after install are normal. They disappear as the water evaporates through the film during curing. If bubbles persist beyond two weeks, contact your installer.

**Cleaning Window Film**

*[Image: Soft cloth being used to clean tinted window interior]*

Clean window film with a soft microfiber cloth and a mild, ammonia-free cleaner. Ammonia is the most common ingredient in household glass cleaners. It attacks the adhesive and the film layers over time, reducing clarity and causing premature failure. Check the label before using any glass cleaner on tinted windows.

Clean with gentle pressure. Do not use abrasive cloths, paper towels, or scrapers on the film surface. These scratch the film coating, which cannot self heal the way PPF does.

**What to Avoid**

*[Image: Warning visual — ammonia cleaners, abrasive materials]*

Do not use:
- Ammonia-based glass cleaners
- Rough cloths or paper products
- Scrapers or hard-edged tools near film edges
- Sharp objects inside the vehicle near the glass

### Section 4: CHARMIST Color Film Care

**Header:** Caring for Your CHARMIST Color Film

**Initial Cure Period**

CHARMIST is a PPF-grade product and follows the same initial cure guidelines as clear PPF. Wait 48 to 72 hours before the first wash. Avoid pressure washing, water exposure, and driving in heavy rain during this window.

**Washing and Maintenance**

*[Image: Hand washing a CHARMIST-wrapped vehicle]*

Hand wash with pH-neutral car wash soap and a clean microfiber mitt. The same rules that apply to clear PPF apply to CHARMIST. Avoid brush car washes, abrasive products, petroleum-based cleaners, and harsh solvents.

**Color Fidelity**

The cast-grade color layer in CHARMIST is stable through normal washing and maintenance. It will not fade, streak, or peel away from the base film under standard care conditions. If color appears to be changing or the film surface looks different in sections, contact your installer for an assessment. Uneven exposure to chemicals or physical damage can cause localized changes that need professional attention.

### Section 5: When to Contact Your Installer

**Header:** When to Contact Your Installer

*[Image: Installer inspecting a vehicle or speaking with a customer]*

Some issues need a trained eye. Contact your MoShiEx certified installer if:

**Edge Lifting**

Any film edge that has started to separate from the panel. The sooner this is addressed, the easier and less costly it is to fix.

**Persistent Bubbles**

Bubbles in PPF or window film that remain more than two weeks after installation. Small bubbles from the installation process are normal and disappear during curing. Bubbles that persist are not.

**Deep Scratches or Cuts**

Marks on the film that go through the coating and into the TPU layer. These will not self heal and may need a section replacement to maintain appearance and protection.

**Contamination Staining**

Stains from bird droppings, tree sap, or industrial fallout that did not come off with normal cleaning. Prolonged exposure to these contaminants can etch the coating layer. A professional can assess whether the stain is on the surface or has penetrated deeper.

**Unexpected Changes in Appearance**

Any area of the film that looks different from surrounding panels, including changes in gloss, color, or clarity that appeared after installation. This can indicate an installation issue, a compatibility issue with a product used on the vehicle, or damage.
