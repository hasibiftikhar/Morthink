### Section 2: Real R&D, Not Just a Factory

**Header:** We Test Every Layer Before It Becomes Your Product

Our AEGOR platform is built from five layers, and each one is engineered and tested on its own before it becomes part of a finished roll.

- **Protective top layer:** Controls clarity, haze, and how cleanly it releases during install
- **Self healing coating:** Resists stains and yellowing, holds its gloss, and heals light scratches without help
- **TPU substrate:** The core layer, built for clarity, UV resistance, and controlled stretch
- **Adhesive layer:** Controls grip strength and clean release if the film is ever removed
- **Release liner:** Protects the adhesive until the moment of install

We adjust the coating and adhesive formula for every product line, since good materials alone do not make a good film. The manufacturing process has to match the material, or performance suffers even when the raw inputs are correct.

We test to real numbers, not guesses. Here is what we hold every batch to:

| What we test | Target | Why it matters for your product |
|---|---|---|
| Elastic modulus | 130 to 450 Kgf/cm² | Controls stretch and positioning during install |
| Tensile strength | 18 MPa or higher | Controls resistance to impact damage |
| Coating elongation at break | 80 percent or higher | Sets the maximum safe stretch during install |
| Film elongation at break | 280 percent or higher | Protects the surface underneath from friction damage |
| Self healing temperature and speed | 45 to 60°C, 5 seconds or less | Controls how fast scratches disappear |
| Installation tack | 15 to 25 N per 25mm | Controls how easily the film wraps around edges |
| Peel strength | 20 to 35 N per 25mm | Controls adhesion strength and clean removal |
| Weather resistance | Color change under 2 delta E, no cracking | Controls how long the film keeps its finish |

We also run real world stress tests before a product ships. In our acid rain resistance test, we apply diluted sulfuric acid and phosphoric acid to a sample, heat it to 75°C for 30 minutes, then rinse and inspect it. In our stain resistance test, we mark a sample with an oil based pen, heat it to 65°C for 24 hours, then try to remove the mark and check for any lasting change. If a sample fails either test, it does not become a product.

## Annotations

- **Headline speaks to the OEM audience specifically**, not the general dealer audience the homepage targets. Your Content Request document lists OEM/ODM brand clients as the second priority audience, right after trade agents, so this page needed its own angle instead of reusing dealer focused language.
- **Section 2 uses your actual R&D process** (five layer construction, coating and adhesive adjustment, real test thresholds) instead of generic claims like "advanced technology" or "state of the art." This is the single strongest differentiator your deck contains, since it shows engineering discipline a competitor's marketing page usually will not show.
- **"Good materials alone do not make a good film" is adapted from your deck's own language** ("Putting good materials together does not necessarily produce good products"), reworded for grade 6 readability while keeping the original point intact.
- **Section 4 leads with traceability**, since OEM buyers care about accountability more than end consumers do. If something goes wrong in a shipment, they need to know Morthink can trace it, not just say the product is good.
- **Section 5 comes directly from your company deck's "Cooperation & Service" slide**, which lists agent recruitment, training, after sales support, tools and materials support, and brand strategy help as your actual service offer. This is real content, not assumed.
- **No pricing appears anywhere**, consistent with your instruction that pricing stays dealer tier or private.
